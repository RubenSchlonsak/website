About Me

Research bringing AI and human activity together through innovative technology

Research

I'm a researcher at Technische Hochschule Lübeck focused on developing innovative sensor technology, especially smart footwear, to create robust algorithms for human activity recognition. My work pushes the boundaries of how AI can understand and interpret human movement patterns in real-world environments.

Expertise

My research interests include real-time activity recognition with multisensory systems, surface identification using capacitive sensing, and applications in healthcare, sports, and autonomous systems. I specialize in bringing AI to edge devices like smart footwear to enable efficient on-device processing.

Beyond Research

When I'm not immersed in research, I find balance through music and creative expression. Playing guitar provides a creative counterpoint to my analytical work, helping me maintain creativity and perspective in my technical pursuits.

Real-Time Sensor Fusion for Smart Footwear

Signal Processing Edge Computing

Develop advanced sensor fusion algorithms for multi-modal data from IMU, pressure, and environmental sensors in smart shoes. Focus on real-time processing, noise reduction, and energy-efficient implementations for wearable devices.

Thesis/Scientific Project

Lightweight CNN Optimization for Wearable Devices

Deep Learning Model Compression

Investigate and implement model compression techniques for CNNs targeting wearable applications. Compare MobileNet variants, quantization methods, and pruning strategies for activity recognition tasks with limited computational resources.

Duration: 3-12 months
Available Now
Thesis/Scientific Project

Self-Learning On-Device Adaptation for Personalized Gait Recognition

Self-Supervised Learning Online Adaptation

Develop a self-supervised, resource-efficient pipeline that continuously adapts gait-recognition models to each wearer using unlabelled IMU and pressure data. Emphasize contrastive pretext tasks, drift detection, and incremental model updates that run in real time on microcontroller-class hardware without cloud connectivity.

Duration: 3–12 months
Available Now

Federated Learning for Wearable Health Monitoring

Privacy AI Healthcare

Design and implement federated learning frameworks for collaborative model training across multiple wearable devices while preserving user privacy. Focus on health monitoring applications and differential privacy techniques.
